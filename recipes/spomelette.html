Spanish potato omelette

The history of this dish

Tortilla de patatas, also known as tortilla española, is one of the most emblematic dishes of Spanish gastronomy.
Its origin is somewhat uncertain, but it is believed to have been invented in the 19th century, sometime during
the Spanish War of Independence. According to popular legend, it was created by an anonymous woman in Navarra to
feed the troops fighting against the French forces, using simple ingredients such as potatoes, eggs and onion.
The recipe spread throughout the country, adapting to local tastes.

A lo largo de los años, la tortilla de patatas ha evolucionado y se ha convertido en un símbolo de la cocina española,
con variantes que incluyen o excluyen la cebolla, o incluso la posibilidad de hacerla con patatas fritas en lugar de
cocidas. En la actualidad, se sirve en todo tipo de ocasiones, desde tapas hasta platos principales, y es un referente
de la comida casera en España. A pesar de sus orígenes humildes, la tortilla de patatas ha perdurado como un plato querido
y representativo de la cultura española.

Ingredients

  • 4 potatoes
  • 2 onions
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • Salt
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Fry the onions in a pan

    2. We begin by poaching the onion in a pan with a little oil and a knob of butter.
    We are going to obtain a caramelized onion that will give a sweet touch to our omelette.

    For that we are going to cook it over medium low heat for 1 hour.
    We turn it from time to time with the lid on, so that it caramelizes.

  2. Poach the potatoes

    3. Once the onion is caramelized, remove it and peel and wash the potatoes.
    We are going to cut them into slices and poach them in a good amount of
    olive oil for about 40 minutes over medium low heat. It should be done
    little by little and the potato.

  3. Mezclar cebollas y patatas con huevos batidos

    4. Add the poached potatoes and onion to the egg and let stand for about 10 minutes.
    At this point add salt and taste. It is important to control the salt at this exact
    moment, later it will be difficult to correct.

  4. Poach the mixture

    5. Now comes the key moment, poach the omelette. We put it in a non-stick pan with a
    drop of oil and we have 30 seconds on each side, nothing more. This is because I like
    it juicy inside but if you like it curdled you have to cook it more.

  5. Serve on a plate

    6. We turn it over and serve. In this way we have made the potato omelette recipe creamy inside.
